Undead

With the exception of the ghost, all undead were once people or animals who became infected with jotnar, the entropy spirits from the heart of the Abyss. In this, they are kin to demons. Unlike demons, the jotnar is in full control. Most of the undead described here were killed before they were infested--the greater undead such as vampires and wights died after infection and were able to maintain some semblance of control or reach a bargain with the entropy spirit. In general, undead are considered extremely dangerous and taboo by most nations--their existence is fueled by draining the aether out of their surroundings. Areas infested by undead become less fertile, starting with young plants and animals. Even the insects die eventually, leaving the area sterile. Extreme exposure can even cause the rocks themselves to crumble into fine grey ash where no life can take root. A very few exceptions exist, where the undead find some other source of energy--a holy vow, a self-sacrificing partner, or powerful elemental magics. These are quite rare, however.

Ghost

Ghosts are unusual for undead--they are not jotnar-infested. There are two general kinds of ghosts--the unsettled spirits of those who could not pass through the veil into Shadow due to obligations, binding magic, or other impediments, and the echoes of the departed. The former will pass on when given the opportunity and when the way is cleared, and often can be reasoned with. The latter aren't really alive at all--they are recordings, shadows etched into reality, brought to life by ambient aetheric conditions (usually traumatic ones), and that must be solved for them to subside. Unlike most undead, ghosts do not generally hate the living. Some want desperately to communicate; others don't even know they are dead.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Rare everywhere. Each one is individual, so only general knowledge can be gained and only with an Intelligence (Arcana or Religion) check.

Habitat: Almost anywhere. Unsettled spirits tend to haunt specific places and echoes occur most often where large-scale magical events (including catastrophes) happened, including battlefields.

Tactics: Ghosts will try to possess people where possible. They only attack if frustrated or antagonized. Often, they prefer to phase through to the Border Shadow to avoid hostilities.

Etherealness.

The ghost enters the Border Shadow from the Material Plane, or vice versa. It is visible on the Material Plane while it is in the Border Ethereal, and vice versa, yet it can't affect or be affected by anything on the other plane.

Horrifying Visage.

Each non-undead creature within 60 feet of the ghost that can see it must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened for 1 minute. If the save fails by 5 or more, the target also ages 1d4 × 10 years. A frightened target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the frightened condition on itself on a success. If a target's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the target is immune to this ghost's Horrifying Visage for the next 24 hours. The aging effect can be reversed with a greater restoration incantation, but only within 24 hours of it occurring.

Possession

One humanoid that the ghost can see within 5 feet of it must succeed on a DC 13 Charisma saving throw or be possessed by the ghost; the ghost then disappears, and the target is incapacitated and loses control of its body. The ghost now controls the body but doesn't deprive the target of awareness. The ghost can't be targeted by any attack, spell, or other effect, except ones that turn undead, and it retains its alignment, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma, and immunity to being charmed and frightened. It otherwise uses the possessed target's statistics, but doesn't gain access to the target's knowledge, class features, or proficiencies.

The possession lasts until the body drops to 0 hit points, the ghost ends it as a bonus action, or the ghost is turned or forced out by an effect like dispel-otherworldly-influence. When the possession ends, the ghost reappears in an unoccupied space within 5 feet of the body. The target is immune to this ghost's Possession for 24 hours after succeeding on the saving throw or after the possession ends.

Ghouls and Ghasts

The ghoul, and its larger cousin the ghast, are rare undead that hunger for flesh and blood as much as simply killing the living. They were infested at death or soon thereafter, retaining much of their physical forms, but becoming withered and growing claws and fangs. The jotnar inside of them drives them to rend and tear, preferring to begin devouring victims alive, consuming body and soul.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Ghouls are uncommon, but widely-known among adventurers. The paralytic nature of their claws are commonly known by anyone with proficiency in History, Religion, or Arcana.

Habitat: Ghouls and ghasts are usually found in heavily-necromantic areas, especially underground crypts. They usually come with other undead or in packs of 3-5, often led by a ghast.

Ghouls and ghasts are straightforward attackers. They prefer to paralyze their victims with their toxic claws before devouring them alive. They will try to drag off paralyzed victims for future consumption. They do not generally have complex schemes, retaining only animal cunning.

Shadow

The shadow is the even-lesser cousin of the specter. Possessing very little of the person whose spirit was consumed to make the shadow, they are malevolent entities with nothing left inside them but hate. Their touch drains the will to live--their victims eventually lie down and give up their lives, often well before their bodies are actually terminally injured.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Shadows are quite uncommon. Distinguishing them from wraiths and specters requires a scholarly background or a successful Intelligence (Arcana) check.

Habitat: Shadows only persist underground or in entirely enclosed areas, and are usually only found in old tombs or crypts. They avoid areas that are regularly exposed to sunlight. When found, they are usually found in packs, often with specters or wraiths in command.

Tactics: Like all incorporeal undead, they prefer to hit and run, using solid objects as cover where possible. They aren't very tactical about it, however, and can be maneuvered into the open with reasonable ease.

Skeleton

Made when jotnar inhabit the bones of an animal or person, skeletons are superior in mentality to zombies, but remain among the least of the undead. Without the rotting flesh to distract, the jotnar can use the encoded, lingering memories to formulate animal-cunning plans. Sometimes they mimic aspects of the person that was before, patrolling where they once were guards, etc. Unlike zombies, they do not usually arise naturally--they tend to be created with intent and given commands, which they execute with precision...as long as they involve killing living things. When not closely supervised, they will twist orders in ways that let them inflict as much suffering and destruction as possible. Unlike golems, the jotnar pulling the strings hate all that lives and constantly works to the ends of the final destruction of all life.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Skeletons are well-known. No checks are needed.

Habitat: Necromancers' or cultist lairs, old crypts. They generally come in large groups with other undead, often with a necromancer or cultist leader.

Tactics: Skeletons use weapons and armor, and will generally act intelligently, at least to some degree. They will not retreat, but they will reposition for better attacks and have been known to pretend to flee to draw unwary adventurers into traps. They often lie around like piles of bones, rising to attack when the adventurers are fully surrounded. Many necromancers prefer skeleton servants to zombies--much more hygenic and able to follow orders, at the cost of being significantly more fragile.

Specter

One of the lesser incorporeal undead, specters are often created from those slain by wraiths or the restless spirits of those who die in heavily necromatically-tainted areas. While they retain sentience, their minds are clouded by a hate for all living things. They exist to drain the life out of everything they touch. Generally, they are under the control of the wraith that created them.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Rare. Distinguishing a specter from a shadow or wraith requires a successful Intelligence (Arcana) check.

Habitat: Specters only persist underground or in entirely enclosed areas, and are usually only found in old tombs or crypts. They avoid areas that are regularly exposed to sunlight. When found, they are usually found in packs, often with wraiths in command or commanding shadows.

Tactics: Like wraiths, they prefer to hide on the other side of solid objects and perform hit and run attacks. They flee from sunlight. They tend to be less focused on a single target than wraiths, attacking opportunistically.

Vampire

It's said that the first vampires were very different from the thirst-driven creatures now known by that name. The Night Lords were early experimenters with imprisoning a jotnar in a cage inside a mortal soul in a quest for power and immortality. This brought extended existence, extreme strength and overwhelming magical and psychic abilities. Thankfully, the last of the Night Lords were destroyed back in the Second Age...or so everyone believes. The ritual that created the Night Lords has been lost, corrupted into the ritual to create vampires. It's said that the White Skull will grant the knowledge of the debased ritual to those that pledge to him but don't have the power to become a true lich.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Legendary. Tales are common...not all the tales are correct. Vampires also are known to mingle in civilization, hiding their identities by spells. Knowledge of weaknesses is common, but much of what is known is exaggerated.

Habitat: Vampires are found in places where mortals gather, as they require sustenance regularly. They often charm their way to the upper reaches of society. They almost always have flunkies and minions and are rarely alone.

Tactics: Vampires rarely play or fight fair. They prefer mind games and hit-and-run attacks, striking from the shadows or manipulating others into doing their bidding. They will generally only engage in direct combat when cornered, and will try to flee (often in mist or bat form) at the first opportunity, as long as their coffin is undisturbed and still hidden. When in combat, they prefer to charm the weakest mind and turn it against its allies. Hostages also are a great tool...and an easy food source. They will grapple and bite whenever possible. It is very rare for a vampire to act alone--they tend to have flunkies (charmed or bought mortals, usually), vampire spawn bound to them, and lesser undead (especially zombies and wights).

If the vampire isn't in sunlight or running water, it can use its action to polymorph into a Tiny bat or a Medium cloud of mist, or back into its true form.

When it drops to 0 hit points outside its resting place, the vampire transforms into a cloud of mist (as in the Shapechanger trait) instead of falling unconscious, provided that it isn't in sunlight or running water. If it can't transform, it is destroyed.

While it has 0 hit points in mist form, it can't revert to its vampire form, and it must reach its resting place within 2 hours or be destroyed. Once in its resting place, it reverts to its vampire form. It is then paralyzed until it regains at least 1 hit point. After spending 1 hour in its resting place with 0 hit points, it regains 1 hit point.

The vampire regains 20 hit points at the start of its turn if it has at least 1 hit point and isn't in sunlight or running water. If the vampire takes radiant damage or damage from holy water, this trait doesn't function at the start of the vampire's next turn.

The vampire can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceilings, without needing to make an ability check.

Otherwise, the effect lasts 24 hours or until the vampire is destroyed, is on a different plane of existence than the target, or takes a bonus action to end the effect.

Vampire Spawn

Master-rank vampires can create spawn, if they choose, by carefully exanguinating their victims and filling them with stolen, jotnar-infused vital fluids. Many, if not most, do not retain much if any of their humanity, rising from the grave as nothing more than hungering monsters, aka ghouls. The strongest retain their sense of self and come to some form of mastery over the jotnar presence, drawing energy from it as it consumes the lives of innocent and guilty alike. Lacking the full powers of a true vampire, as well as its ability to vanish into mist and come back to unlife, spawn are dangerous nonetheless. Spawn do inherit some of the weaknesses of their parents.

Spawn can reason and think like the people they were, but for most of them their living passions and interests start to wither, leaving only the blood-thirst and taste for inflicting pain. Rare spawn retain their ability to use magic or their pre-transformation personality and desires.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Vampire spawn, like vampires, are legendary. Confused tales exist, many false and spread by vampires themselves.

Habitat: Vampire spawn are generally found in the lair of a master-rank vampire; occasionally they will terrorize a countryside or town when their creator lets them loose or loses control.

Tactics: Vampire spawn tend to come in packs and attack from ambush, using their Spider Climb ability to get around. They are not mindless--they will break off when threatened with their vulnerabilities, but will tend to regroup and attack again. They will usually try to grapple and bite humanoids, especially if they can isolate them. Vampire spawn tend to work in concert with other undead, but usually only lesser ones such as ghouls, wights, and zombies.

Wight

One of the least of the greater undead, wights were infested with jotnar before their deaths, whether by living and dying in demon-traversed areas or by being sacrificed to demons. Some of the greater undead practice the creation of wights under their control. They retain much of the skills and knowledge of their living self, shrouded under the hunger and hatred attendant to being undead. They can drain the life out of their foes at a touch, weakening them and making them vulnerable to becoming undead themselves while healing the wight.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Of the greater undead, wights are among the more well-known. Only ignorant or naive adventurers need to make Intelligence (Arcana) checks for information.

Habitat: Found wherever undead are. Generally in groups of 3-5 or commanding swarms of lesser corporeal undead.

Tactics: Wights are smarter than zombies or skeletons and not as ruled by hunger as ghouls. As a result, they tend to use actual tactics, preferring to pick off the ones they consider dangerous from safety. If pressed into melee, they will focus on a single target until they've killed them with their Life Drain. They tend to run in packs with other wights or zombies, especially including zombies under their control. They frequently are found under the command of a greater undead such as a vampire, lich, or other such dominating creature, acting as sergeants directing squads of lesser undead.

Will-o'-Wisp

The will-o'-wisp is a rarity--a jotnar-infested nature spirit. With a combination of the fey's trickster nature and the jotnari hatred for life, they delight in luring travelers and animals to their doom, miring them in the swamps they call home before burning them to death with malevolent lightning and consuming their souls. Thankfully, they are quite rare, known only in a few areas in the Sundown Hills and in the Hagfen.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Uncommon. Those familiar with swamplands and the kami know their patterns automatically; everyone else requires an Intelligence (Nature or Arcana) check.

Habitat: Swamplands, especially necrotically-active ones. Generally found in groups of 2-4, and rarely with other creatures.

Tactics: Will-o'-wisps tend to act in groups. They prefer trickery, luring enemies through dangerous (and especially ensnaring) terrain before clustering to feed. They frequently go invisible to gain advantage or to confuse their foes.

Wraith

As the most powerful of the incorporeal undead, wraiths are thankfully quite rare. They result when a trapped spirit, unable to cross over into Shadow, becomes infested with jotnar while still full of mortal vigor. They remember much of who they were…but are suffused and controlled by the jotnari hunger and hatred for all living things. They pass the infection on to those that die near them, converting them into shadowy undead slaves. Due to their incorporeal nature, they are difficult to hurt either with magic or steel, except for abilities that channel the radiant nature of the Astral plane.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Rare. They try to disguise themselves as lesser incorporeal undead where they can, so distinguishing them from others is difficult.

Habitat: As with other incorporeal undead, they are only found in the depths of lightless crypts and other such areas. Usually commanding groups of shadows or specters.

Tactics: Wraiths tend to act in concert with lesser incorporeal undead. They will attack by phasing through solid objects and then running away, harrying their opponents to death. They are smart enough to plan tactics, rather than simply attack the first they see. They tend to focus on one target until killed, then use their Create Specter ability.

Zombies

The lowest, least intelligent and least independent of the undead, the zombie is a simple putrefying corpse reanimated by a jotnar, whether spontaneously or summoned by a necromantic ritual. No coherent thoughts remain in their rotting flesh and their bodies lack even a trace of the soul that once brought them to life. Unless forcibly compelled by blackest magic, zombies will attack any living thing--not out of hunger, but out of unrelenting hate for for all living existence. In the absence of anything to attack and when left without orders, they simply stand there, doing nothing. Zombies do cling to false-life with a tenacity nearly unmatched by other undead. Unless dismembered or burned in radiant fire, they frequently get up from even massive damage.

Presented here are two types of zombies, but any animal or person that once lived can be zombified. Zombies all have abysmal mental ability scores and poor Dexterity, regardless of what creature they came from, while their Strength and Constitution tend to improve slightly. They all have the Undead Fortitude trait, but never have proficiency in weapons or armor.

Habitat and Tactics

Commonality: Common. Of all the undead, these are the best-known. No checks are necessary.

Habitat: Anywhere. They are the most likely to spontaneously arise from improper burial in unconsecrated ground. Old battlefields are often rife with them. And rarely will a single zombie arise by itself--where you see one zombie, there are probably dozens.

Tactics: Zombies don't have tactics beyond charging at the nearest living creature and using their Slam attack until the creature is dead or they are destroyed themselves. They will move blindly through dangerous terrain or spell effects, do not coordinate, and will frequently get in each other's way. It's all a controlling creature can do to get them pointed in the right direction; when uncontrolled they will blindly attack. Uncontrolled zombies are frequently either alone or in combination with other lesser undead such as ghouls or wights; zombies also serve as the basic brute soldier of more organized necromantic armies.
